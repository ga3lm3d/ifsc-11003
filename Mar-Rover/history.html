Mission History

NASA's Mars rover missions show how robotic exploration has grown from short-term surface missions into long-running mobile science laboratories.

Launch and Landing Timeline

2003–2004

Spirit and Opportunity

Spirit launched in June 2003 and Opportunity launched in July 2003. Both rovers landed on Mars in January 2004. Their goal was to search for clues about the history of water on Mars by studying rocks, minerals, and terrain.

Spirit explored Gusev Crater, while Opportunity explored Meridiani Planum. Opportunity became famous for lasting far longer than its planned mission.

2011–2012

Curiosity

Curiosity launched in November 2011 and landed in Gale Crater in August 2012. Its mission was to determine whether Mars ever had conditions that could support microbial life.

Curiosity carries advanced instruments for analyzing rocks, soil, atmosphere, and radiation. It has helped scientists understand ancient Martian environments.

2020–2021

Perseverance

Perseverance launched in July 2020 and landed in Jezero Crater in February 2021. Jezero was selected because scientists believe it once held a lake and river delta.

Perseverance searches for signs of ancient microbial life, studies Martian geology, and collects rock and regolith samples for possible return to Earth by a future mission.

Mission Goals and Accomplishments

Spirit and Opportunity

Mission Goals

The twin rovers were designed to act as robotic geologists. Their main goal was to study Martian rocks and soil to understand whether water had once existed on the surface.

Major Accomplishments

The rovers found strong geologic evidence that water once affected areas of Mars. Opportunity discovered minerals and rock formations that helped scientists understand ancient wet environments.

Technical Features

Spirit and Opportunity used solar power, panoramic cameras, microscopic imagers, rock abrasion tools, and instruments for studying minerals and chemistry.

Curiosity

Mission Goals

Curiosity was built to investigate whether Mars once had the chemical ingredients and environmental conditions needed to support microbial life.

Major Accomplishments

Curiosity found evidence of ancient habitable environments, including clues that Gale Crater once had water and conditions that could have supported microbes.

Technical Features

Curiosity includes a drill, cameras, chemistry instruments, environmental sensors, and a radioisotope power system that allows it to operate through dust and seasonal changes.

Perseverance

Mission Goals

Perseverance focuses on astrobiology, including the search for signs of ancient microbial life. It also collects samples for possible future return to Earth.

Major Accomplishments

Perseverance has studied Jezero Crater's rocks, explored evidence related to water and habitability, and collected carefully selected Martian samples.

Technical Features

Perseverance carries advanced cameras, a sample caching system, scientific instruments, microphones, improved wheels, and technology demonstrations that support future exploration.
