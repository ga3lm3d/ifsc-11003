Exploring Mars One Rover at a Time

NASA's Mars rovers are robotic explorers designed to investigate the surface of Mars, study its rocks and soil, search for evidence of past water, and help scientists understand whether Mars may once have supported microbial life.

View Mission Timeline

Program Overview

Mars rover missions are part of NASA's long-term effort to understand the Red Planet. Each rover carries scientific instruments, cameras, mobility systems, and communication tools that allow mission teams on Earth to study Mars from millions of miles away.

These missions also help prepare for future exploration by testing landing systems, navigation methods, sample collection techniques, and technologies that may one day support human missions to Mars.

Featured Rovers

Spirit and Opportunity

Spirit and Opportunity were twin rovers that landed on Mars in 2004. Their mission focused on studying rocks and soil for evidence that water once shaped the Martian environment.

  • Launch: 2003
  • Landing: 2004
  • Major focus: Evidence of past water

Curiosity

Curiosity is a car-sized rover that landed in Gale Crater in 2012. It studies Martian geology and investigates whether Mars once had environmental conditions suitable for microbial life.

  • Launch: 2011
  • Landing: 2012
  • Major focus: Habitability

Perseverance

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater in 2021. Its mission includes searching for signs of ancient life and collecting rock and regolith samples for possible future return to Earth.

  • Launch: 2020
  • Landing: 2021
  • Major focus: Astrobiology and sample collection
